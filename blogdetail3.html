Beginner Tips for Starting Kuchipudi Online

Back to All Blogs

Published on: January 22, 2025 • By Mayuri Kuchipudi Academy

Beginning your Kuchipudi journey online is a wonderful step toward embracing classical art from the comfort of your home. With dedication, discipline and structured learning, beginners can build a strong foundation that supports advanced training in the future.

1. Create a Dedicated Practice Space

Choose a calm room with enough space for movement. Good lighting, a clean floor surface, and a device positioned at eye level will bring a more classroom-like experience to online sessions.

2. Focus on Posture and Basic Stances

Beginners should learn Samapadam, Araimandi and basic foot positions. A correct posture prevents injuries and brings elegance to expressions and movements in later stages.

3. Build Discipline Through Consistent Practice

Classical dance grows through repetition. Even 20–30 minutes daily of practice can build stamina, balance and confidence. Use slow warm-ups before starting any routine.

4. Strengthen Rhythm & Footwork

Practice talam exercises using a metronome or simple clapping drills. Keeping rhythm is essential for performance accuracy and confidence.

5. Learn With Patience & Progress Gradually

Kuchipudi is not just movement—it is a spiritual and expressive art. Progress may feel slow in the beginning, but growth becomes visible with disciplined effort and proper guidance.
